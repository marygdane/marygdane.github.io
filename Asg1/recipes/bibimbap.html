Bibimbap - Korean Mixed Rice with Meat and Assorted Vegetables

Image courtesy of and recipe adapted from My Korean Kitchen

Bibimbap (비빔밥) is probably one of the most well-known and beloved Korean dishes to many people. Even if people don’t know anything about Korea, it’s not too difficult to find people who had Bibimbap sometime in their lives.

Bibimbap simply translates to “mixed rice with meat and assorted vegetables“. You can make endless variations to this dish depending on your preference and dietary requirements (e.g. Saessak (micro leaves) Bibimbap) and also based on the dish/bowl it is served in (e.g. Dolsot (hot stone bowl) Bibimbap and Yangpun Bibimbap).

It is typically served with a spicy red pepper sauce, and this recipe for the sauce is absolutely delicious!

Servings 3 to 4
Prep Time 35 minutes
Cook Time 55 minutes
Total Time 1 hour 30 minutes

Ingredients

Meat

  • 1 pound ground pork (or any other preferred meat)
  • 4 tbsp soy sauce
  • 4 tbsp sesame oil
  • 4 tsp sugar - I used brown sugar
  • 1 tsp minced garlic

Accompaniments

  • 1 red bell pepper (thinly sliced)
  • ½ of a daikon radish (julienned)
  • 100 g shiitake mushrooms (thinly sliced)
  • 2 carrots (julienned)
  • salt & pepper to taste
  • 2 cups steamed rice (3 to 4 serving portions)
  • 3-4 eggs (depending on the serving portion)
  • Cooking oil
  • Some Korean seasoned seaweed shredded (long thin cut)

Bibimbap sauce

  • 2 tbsp gochujang
  • 1 tbsp sesame oil
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • 1 tbsp water
  • 1 tbsp roasted sesame seeds
  • 1 tsp vinegar (I use rice vinegar)
  • 1 tsp minced garlic

Instructions

  1. Mix the meat ingredients together in a bowl, and marinate the meat for about 30 mins while you are working on other ingredients to enhance the flavour.
  2. Mix the Bibimbap sauce ingredients in a bowl, and set aside.
  3. Add some cooking oil and a pinch of fine sea salt in a wok and cook the carrots on medium high to high heat for 3 to 4 mins.
  4. Add some cooking oil and a pinch of fine sea salt in a wok and cook the daikon on medium high to high heat for 3 to 4 mins.
  5. Add some cooking oil and a pinch of fine sea salt in a wok and cook the mushrooms on medium high to high heat for 2 to 3 mins.
  6. Add some cooking oil and a pinch of fine sea salt in a wok and cook the red bell pepper on medium high to high heat for 2 to 3 mins.
  7. Add some cooking oil into a wok and cook the meat on medium high to high heat for 3 to 5 mins, or until fully cooked.
  8. Make fried eggs to your preference.
  9. Put the rice into a bowl and add the meat, assorted vegetables, seasoned seaweed, Bibimbap sauce and the egg on top of the rice. Serve.
  10. Mix the ingredients well in the bowl and enjoy!
