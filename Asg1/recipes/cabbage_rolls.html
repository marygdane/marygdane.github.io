Cabbage Rolls

Image courtesy of Shizuoka Gourmet and recipe adapted from Slow Cooker Adventures

What I love most about this recipe is how the lemon brings out the flavors of the fresh tomatoes inside each cabbage roll and the cabbage takes on a "sweetness!" They are so yummy!

It does take a long time to make though, so if you do not have the time but want the flavours, please see the notes on an alternate method of making this recipe.

Servings 30 rolls
Prep Time 1 hour
Cook Time 6 hours
Total Time 7 hours

Ingredients

  • 1 head of Green Cabbage
  • 1 lb. ground beef
  • 1 cup uncooked rice
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 2 large tomatoes, chopped
  • ½ cup parsley, finely chopped
  • ¼ cup Lemon Juice
  • ¼ cup Olive Oil
  • 1 ½ tsp. salt
  • 1 tsp. pepper
  • 2 tsp. garlic powder

Instructions

  1. First, brown the ground beef with the onions. Once it is cooked, combine all the remaining "filling" ingredients in a big bowl.
  2. Next, peel off the outer layers of the head of cabbage that are dirty. Cut the stem off as best you can. Either parboil or steam your head of cabbage.
  3. As the head steams or parboils, peel off the outer layer of leaves very carefully as they become soft enough. Usually you can get 2-3 leaves off before having to wait some more.
  4. Once all you have all your leaves, you will need to thin the vein in each leaf. Turn the leaf with the "bumpy side" up on your cutting board.
  5. Next, starting at the thin part of the vein, run the tip of your knife very carefully toward the thickest part, thinning the vein so it makes the leaf more pliable.
  6. Once all the leaves' veins are thinned, it's time to stuff them. Place the leaf so that the leaf curves upward, like a cup. Place about 3-4 Tbsp. of filling into the leaf (depending on the size of the leaf, use your judgement. It may hold more of less!)
  7. Next, fold the horizontal (left and right) ends in first.
  8. Then, fold the top over toward the bottom.
  9. Lastly, fold the bottom up toward the top to complete your beautiful and delicious little cabbage package!
  10. Make layers in the bottom of your slow cooker of TIGHTLY packed cabbage rolls.
  11. Cover the rolls with water, but not too much that the cabbage rolls are "floating".

Notes

If you do not have the time to make cabbage rolls, opt for a cabbage casserole instead.
Simply follow step 1, then chop the cabbage into whatever size you desire, and mix everything in a slow cooker with enough water to be just below the surface of your ingredients.
